## The Challenge

The client was experiencing stalled growth despite significant investment in sales and marketing. Their go-to-market motion had evolved organically over five years, resulting in fragmented territories, inconsistent messaging, and unclear handoffs between teams.

## Approach

We started with a comprehensive audit of the existing GTM motion, interviewing stakeholders across sales, marketing, customer success, and product. The goal was to understand not just what was broken, but why.

### Phase 1: Discovery & Analysis

- Mapped the current buyer journey across all touchpoints
- Analyzed win/loss data to identify patterns
- Segmented the customer base by value and behavior
- Audited competitive positioning and messaging

### Phase 2: Architecture Design

Based on our findings, we designed a new GTM architecture with:

- **Clear segment definitions** aligned to buyer personas and use cases
- **Territory model** based on opportunity potential rather than geography alone
- **Unified messaging framework** with segment-specific adaptations
- **Stage-gated handoff process** between marketing and sales

### Phase 3: Implementation

We worked closely with sales leadership to roll out the new model in phases:

1. Pilot with two high-performing reps
2. Iterate based on feedback
3. Full team rollout with enablement
4. Ongoing optimization cadence

## Results

Within six months of full implementation, the client saw:

- **3.2x pipeline growth** from improved targeting
- **28% improvement in win rate** from better qualification
- **15-day reduction in sales cycle** from clearer handoffs

## Key Learnings

The most impactful change wasn't the territory model or the messaging—it was getting alignment on definitions. Once everyone agreed on what a qualified opportunity looked like, everything else fell into place.
